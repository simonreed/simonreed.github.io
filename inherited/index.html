Are you an agency that's just inherited a client with a Rails site, but you don't have Rails developers in-house?

It's OK — you're not the first, and you won't be the last.

I've worked with many agencies in the same boat. Instead of taking on the financial risk of hiring a full-time Rails developer in-house, they've turned to me.

I've worked on a wide range of projects, small and large. My clients include bootstrap startups, VC-backed startups, and creative agencies.

Experience when you need it

While I have a wealth of experience developing complex systems in Ruby and Rails, you don't need to be tied down to a lengthy and costly contract or permanent employment. My clients enjoy a level of flexibility that means I can scale my involvement up and down to suit their needs. Have a sudden deadline? Let me know — and once your workload goes back to normal, I'll go back into hiatus, ready for you when you need me next.

Keep the client happy — without another full-time hire

Code and features are great, but that's not the whole job of a developer. We need to be able to liaise with the client and meet — and exceed — their expectations.

When you hire me to work on your project, you're not just hiring another set of hands. You're hiring someone to guide the client throughout the project from inception to completion.

Got an existing project that needs to meet its objectives?

I often have clients approach me needing help on an existing project that isn't going to plan. Whether it's a startup founder having trouble with their codebase as their team grows, or a business owner spending most of the day inside their own admin section — I can review the project and tell you what steps are required to get it back on track.

I make people more money. I don't care about writing code for the sake of it; all I care about is how much I can make you. I'll analyse the business, find the areas that need improvement, and come up with a game plan.

In this for the long haul

One of the biggest issues people find when working with consultants is that they flake or quit to join another company full-time. Not with me.

I'm not just running a side business until a full-time job comes my way. I want to build strong, long-term relationships with great people. You can rest easy knowing I'll always be around.
